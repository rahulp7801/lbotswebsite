City Shapers

About our Project
As we are located in California, droughts and wildfires are common. We decided that we wanted to find a solution that can detect wildfires in an early stage and prevent them from becoming large mega-fires that devastated our state last year.
01
Cost-Effective
The solution was a one-time cost for the city, and was not very out of budget.
02
Community
Our solution also integrated the community with fighting wildfires. We designed an app that provides incentives for being on the lookout.
03
Reliable
Our solution had a multitude of ways to detect the fire and put it out at an early stage, including satellites such as NOAA's GOES satellite.
So even if one method was faulty, there would always be another option, so uptime would be pretty much 100%.

Solution sharing

We shared our solution with our local firefighters, as well as the founder of the NOAA GOES satellite, Alexander Koltunov. After receiving excellent feedback, we tweaked our project to make it be where it is now.

Into Orbit

About our Project
As we are located in California, droughts and wildfires are common. We decided that we wanted to find a solution that can detect wildfires in an early stage and prevent them from becoming large mega-fires that devastated our state last year.
01
Feasible
RNA and DNA editing had recently been used to create a sheep. Therefore, it would not be hard to imagine doing the same for a plant.
02
Long-Term Benefits
Our solution would allow for faster greenhousing of Mars and other extraterrestrial bodies. By editing the very "code" of the plants, we can "program" them in a way that would allow them to adapt quickly to the harsh environment outside of Earth.
03
Cost-Effective
Instead of spending billions of dollars on transporting and maintaining large greenhouses, by editing the DNA of a single plant, we can save billions while accomplishing the same thing, if not with better results.

Solution sharing

We shared our solution with multiple UC Davis (Univesity of California, Davis) professors and students who specialized in astrophysics. We learned that a major challenge with going past Earth was keeping food readily available. We decided to tackle this problem.
